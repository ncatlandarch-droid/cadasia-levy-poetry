2 Ownership & Intellectual Property

The Participant owns all creative works including poems, artwork, audio recordings, and the "Words That Heal" brand name. The Foundation holds zero equity and zero ownership in the Participant's intellectual property.

What you own forever: All poems & written works · All original artwork · The "Words That Heal" brand · Your name, likeness & persona · All audio recordings of your poetry.

The Participant grants the Foundation a revocable, non-exclusive license to display and sell the Participant's creative works through the platform. This license may be revoked at any time with 30 days written notice.

3 Revenue Model

Revenue from merch sales and digital products is split as follows:

Revenue StreamCreator ShareFoundation Share
Merchandise sales (t-shirts, mugs, prints)70% of net profit30% of net profit
Digital products (audio collection, ebook)70% of net profit30% of net profit
Tips & donations (CashApp, Zelle)100% — directly to Creator0%
Plain English: You earn 70% of profit on everything sold through the store. Tips go straight to you — 100%. The Foundation's 30% covers platform costs, hosting, marketing, and fulfillment. Revenue is calculated and paid monthly by the 5th business day via CashApp ($cadasiata) or Zelle. A monthly sales report is provided with all transactions. Minimum payout: $10 (smaller amounts roll to next month).

4 Participant Obligations

Participant agrees that:

(a) All information provided is truthful and accurate;

(b) They are the sole creator of the works submitted for display and sale;

(c) They will continue creating new poems and artwork — content is the engine of the brand;

(d) They will approve merch designs before production;

(e) They will promote their website through social media and personal networks;

(f) They will respond to communications within 48 hours;

(g) They will not misrepresent the Think! Ventures relationship.

5 What You Don't Have To Do

The Foundation handles all technical and business operations. The Participant is not responsible for:

• Managing the website • Handling customer orders • Processing payments • Dealing with technology • Paying any platform fees • Shipping merchandise

6 Engagement & Archival

Participants who show no activity for 90 consecutive days and do not respond to two outreach attempts will be moved to Inactive status (reduced support). After 180 days of inactivity, services may be archived with 30 days prior notice. Reactivation is available at any time by contacting the Foundation.

7 Termination

Either party may terminate this agreement with 30 days written notice. The Foundation may terminate immediately for cause including illegal activity, fraud, harassment, or misrepresentation.

Upon termination:

• Website stays live for 30 days for transition

• All creative content returned to the Participant

• Pending revenue paid within 15 business days

• Merch products removed from the store

• No penalties. No fees. No strings.

8 Limitation of Liability

The Foundation provides creative platform services, not legal or financial advice. The Participant is responsible for their own creative and business decisions. The Foundation is not liable for business outcomes. Participants are encouraged to consult an attorney for legal matters and a CPA for tax matters.

9 Case Study Permission

By signing this agreement, Participant grants the Foundation permission to feature their brand as a case study in marketing materials, grant applications, and public communications. The Foundation will seek approval before publication and will anonymize details upon request.

10 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Foundation and the Participant regarding the services described herein. It may be amended only in writing signed by both parties.
